About Me

I am a Ph.D. candidate in computer science at the University of California, Riverside, advised by Professor Mohsen Lesani. My research interests are primarily in theoretical distributed computing and systems. I study both protocols and limitations across different distributed settings. I have worked on blockchains, heterogeneous quorum systems, and replication systems.

Currently, my focus is on personalization and localization in distributed systems.

I am also interested in graph theory, formal verification, complexity theory, game theory, and algorithms.
